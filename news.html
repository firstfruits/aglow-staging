Article in Howick Pakuranga Times

9th February 2015 CATERING FOR LITTLIES:

Laraine Bennett, president of East Auckland Aglow, is glad of C3 Church senior pastor Wayne Peat’s offer of crèche accommodation for the women’s group monthly meetings. Times photo Wayne Martin FORMER kindergarten teacher Laraine Bennett, conscious of the need to provide environments conducive to mums and their littlies, has found a new home for a women’s group that will accommodate a crèche.

Loraine is president of East Auckland Aglow, part of an international multi-denominational network that gathers monthly for meetings and prayer groups. Mrs Bennett says the group provides encouragement, support and friendship for women of all denominations. While the movement was started in New Zealand 40 years ago, the East Auckland group is just two-and-a-half years old and until now its members have met at Te Tuhi in Pakuranga. But the contemplative nature of the arts and boisterous worship do not always mix. And littlies running about the room can be a distraction for young mums looking to nurture their spiritual and their emotional development. Mrs Bennett decided a crèche was needed.

Enter Wayne Peat, a C3 Church senior pastor, who was guest speaker at an Aglow meeting. On hearing of Aglow’s dilemma he offered the facilities at his Highland Park church, already well geared up for mums and their little ones. “The music is all here – contemporary and meaningful worship facilities which allow us to be more upmarket.” Mrs Bennett joined Aglow in 1978, by which time her children were older. But, she says, as a former kindergarten teacher, she retains an intense interest in young mums and music. “I like my contact with the older people as well,” she says. “It’s important for mums with young children to be able to inter-relate with the older ones and hear their stories. “We encourage women of all ages to join us. But we can’t have the little ones running around the room, hence the need to have a crèche so that the mothers can relax.” Pastor Peat says a sense of family is generated, “a cross-generational activity where people can give support to each other”. “I felt we could provide a safe environment for women engaged in their spiritual and emotional development. “This organisation [Aglow] helps them on the journey and some good life transformational stuff takes place.” Mrs Bennett says women of all ages are invited to share “our worship, stories, tears and laughter, and meet other women from our surrounding suburbs”.

Barbara Woodcock

It is with great delight, pleasure and excitement we welcome onto the Aglow NZ National Board Barbara Woodcock. Yes we now have 5 on the National Board: what an amazing start to this new year and new decade. Barbara has served within Aglow as Local President and also Area President. In recent years has attended Aglow National Conferences, our Leadership Summits and been a great support to the National Board. Barbara will be in the National Office 3 days a week serving as PA to the National President. And, will also handle some Finance (releasing Glenda) and will be the new Membership and Affiliation person (releasing Yvonne)

John McGregor

First 'male' on a Local Fellowship Board: Many years ago the Lord connected John McGregor to Whangarei Aglow, it was soon discovered he came with many additional gifts. John has been a wonderful Advisor and blessed Liz, local President/NFR and the Aglow over many years. John is also very involved in the Northern Region gatherings, has a heart for sharing the Kingdom of God. Last October the Holy Spirit said there was more and John was invited onto the Whangarei Aglow Local Board. In November while Yvonne, Glenda and I were up North we anointed John and welcomed him into this new position.

Kay Payne

In the months leading up to when Sharon Hill's term as NFR for Lower South would finish, it was suggested that Lower South return to its original state of being two Regions. Following good discussions with Leaders it is with great delight that we confirm that this move has been made and in turn this has opened the door for us to welcome two wonderful new NFRs: Kay Payne, NFR - Lower South East. Kay was a Member on Sharon’s NFR Team and prior to this served as President of Mosgiel.

Lynn Dodds

Lynn Dodds: NFR - Lower South West. Lynn was also previously on the NFR Team with Sharon. Prior to that appointment Lynn was President of Hokonui.

Tribute to Rita Restall, Aglow International NZ’s first president. Aglow International New Zealand's first president went home to be with her Lord at the beginning of March 2015.
